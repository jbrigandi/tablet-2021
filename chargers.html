Los Angeles Chargers Overview

The Los Angeles Chargers are a professional American football franchise based in the Los Angeles metropolitan area. The Chargers compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member club of the league's American Football Conference (AFC) West division. The Chargers play their home games at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California, which the club shares with the Los Angeles Rams. The club was founded in 1960, and spend their first season in Los Angeles before relocating to San Diego in 1961. In 2017, the team moved back to Los Angeles, after 56 seasons in San Diego. The team's owner is Dean Spanos, the team's head coach is Brandon Staley, and the team's general manager is Tom Telesco.

Los Angeles Chargers History

The Chargers were established with seven other American Football League teams in 1959. They began AFL play in Los Angeles the following year in 1960. The Chargers' original owner was hotel heir Barron Hilton, son of Hilton Hotels founder Conrad Hilton. In the 1994 season, the Chargers made their first and, so far, only Super Bowl appearance, against the 49ers in Super Bowl XXIX. Chargers' Current Leaders

For the 2021 Season, the current passing leader is Justin Herbert with 1,994 passing yards and 16 passing touchdowns, the current rushing leader is Austin Ekeler with 420 rushing yards and 5 rushing touchdowns, and the receiving leader is Mike Williams with 517 receiving yards and 6 receiving touchdowns. On the defensive side of the ball, Derwin James is leading the team with 63 tackles, Joey Bosa is leading the team in sacks with 3.5 sacks, and Asante Samuel Jr. is leading the team in interceptions with 2 interceptions.
